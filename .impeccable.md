## Design Context

### Users
Solo developers and indie hackers building AI agents who need automated Instagram marketing to drive traffic to their products (Stan Store, Skool, Shopify, Calendly). They're technical enough to use a CLI and API, but they're buying a done-for-you outcome — not a tool to tinker with. They care about results (clicks, traffic) not dashboards. Price-sensitive at $297-697/mo, so trust and credibility matter immediately.

### Brand Personality
**Direct. Confident. No-BS.**

The voice is a competent peer who doesn't oversell. Short sentences. No hype words. Says what it does, shows the proof, gets out of the way. Think: the person at the meetup who built something that actually works and explains it in 30 seconds.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Industrial/utilitarian — light mode.** Raw, functional, confidence through simplicity. The design should feel like infrastructure: reliable, no-nonsense, built to work. Not decorative. Not playful. Not corporate.

- **Theme**: Light mode only. Warm whites, not clinical. Tinted neutrals.
- **References**: Stripe's clarity (not its polish), Linear's density, Vercel's confidence — but rougher, less precious. More workshop, less showroom.
- **Anti-references**: Glowing dark-mode developer tools. Gradient text. Fake terminal windows with macOS dots. Identical card grids. Anything that screams "AI generated this landing page."
- **Typography**: Functional, not decorative. One strong sans-serif. Monospace reserved strictly for actual code/commands.
- **Color**: One distinctive brand color used sparingly as a functional accent (links, active states, CTAs). Not blue-600. Neutrals do the heavy lifting.

### Design Principles

1. **Earn every element.** If it doesn't help the user decide or act, remove it. Decoration is debt.
2. **Structure communicates.** Layout, spacing, and hierarchy do the selling — not color or effects. If you turned the page grayscale, the hierarchy should still be obvious.
3. **Confidence is quiet.** Bold claims in plain containers. No visual shouting. The copy is already confident — the design should let it breathe.
4. **Functional beauty.** Beauty comes from precision: perfect alignment, intentional spacing, typographic rhythm. Not from gradients, glows, or animations.
5. **Break the template.** No two sections should use the same layout. Vary alignment, spacing, and composition. Asymmetry is intentional, not accidental.
